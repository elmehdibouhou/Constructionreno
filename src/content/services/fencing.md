faqs:
  - q: "How much does fence installation cost in Ontario?"
    a: "Fence installation in Ontario typically costs $25–$60 per linear foot installed, depending on fence type and height. A standard 150 linear foot vinyl privacy fence runs $4,000–$8,000. Cedar wood fencing costs $30–$55 per linear foot. We provide a written estimate after a free on-site measurement."
  - q: "Do I need a permit to install a fence in Ontario?"
    a: "Fence permit requirements vary by municipality. In most Ontario cities, fences under 2 metres (6.5 ft) on residential properties do not require a permit, but setback rules and neighbour agreements may apply. Pool enclosures always require a permit. We guide you through the process and can handle permit applications."
  - q: "What is the most popular fence type in Ontario?"
    a: "Vinyl (PVC) privacy fencing is the most popular choice for Ontario backyards — it's maintenance-free, never rots or splinters, handles freeze-thaw cycles well, and stays looking crisp white for decades. Cedar wood is the top choice for homeowners wanting a natural, warm aesthetic."
  - q: "How deep do fence posts need to be in Ontario?"
    a: "In Ontario, fence posts must be set below the frost line — a minimum of 4 feet (48 inches) deep for most of the province. We use concrete-set posts at the correct depth to prevent frost heaving. Shortcuts on post depth are the #1 cause of fence failure in Canada."
  - q: "Can you install a fence on a slope or uneven terrain?"
    a: "Yes — we handle sloped lots using either a racked (follows the slope) or stepped (level panels at different heights) installation depending on fence type and your preference. We assess your terrain during the free estimate."
